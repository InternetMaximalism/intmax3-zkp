## 5. F-WD-2 fix (Option B) ‚Äî applied and verified (2026-07-04)

The one OPEN exploitable finding from ¬ß3, F-WD-2, was **fixed and end-to-end
verified**, following the CLAUDE.md discipline for proof-logic/protocol changes.

- **Root cause.** `Transfer` has no salt; the withdrawal/receive nullifier
  `Poseidon(SettledTransfer)` keyed its uniqueness on the *settlement*
  `block_number` (`send_leaf.cur`). Settlement (`update_channel_tree.rs`) does
  not gate a tx by nonce (ChannelLeaf has no settled-nonce field), so a block
  producer / channel co-signer could settle one sender tx into two blocks ‚Üí
  two distinct nullifiers for one deduction ‚Üí double native withdrawal AND
  double receive-credit. Impact capped by `solvent_from_genesis` (peer-escrow
  theft, not unbounded inflation), but not proven safe.
- **Fix (Option B).** Re-key the `SettledTransfer` preimage last field from
  `block_number` (BlockNumber/U63, 1 limb) to the sender `tx.nonce` (u32, 1
  limb) ‚Äî same preimage length. `nonce` is a settlement-INDEPENDENT, one-time
  identifier bound to the deduction (`spend_circuit.rs` requires the sent-tx
  slot at index=nonce empty before write + sequential nonce; `tx_settlement.rs`
  binds settled `tx_v2.nonce == tx.nonce`). Two settlements of one deduction
  now yield the IDENTICAL nullifier ‚Üí the existing on-chain
  `withdrawalNullifierUsed` set and the recipient indexed-merkle reject the
  second use. Changed: `src/common/transfer.rs`, `single_withdrawal_circuit.rs`,
  `receive_transfer_circuit.rs`, `balance_witness_generator.rs`, and the C14
  soundness test. NO Solidity change (nullifier is opaque 32B on-chain).
- **Process.** Threat model (`tasks/F-WD-2-threatmodel.md`) ‚Üí attacker
  red-team of the *design* (GO for B; it also caught that the originally-planned
  Option A "strictly-increasing settled nonce" is a **liveness bug** ‚Äî
  settlement is non-contiguous ‚Äî so corrected-A is a per-channel
  "nonce-not-previously-settled" SET, deferred as optional defense-in-depth) ‚Üí
  implementation (implementer ‚âÝ reviewer) ‚Üí adversarial review of the *code*
  (GO; nonce provably bound at all 4 sites, native/target preimage identical,
  swap complete, ordering invariants intact, tests not weakened).
- **Lean closure.** `SingleWithdrawalCircuit.lean` `wNul` re-keyed to
  `settledNullifier ‚Ä¶ tx.nonce` (already a bound wire via `sentTx`);
  `withdrawal_sound` re-proved; `EndToEnd.lean` single-use clause (d) re-argued
  from nonce-uniqueness (not settlement-uniqueness); F-WD-2 moved out of the
  RESIDUAL TRUST SURFACE. `lake build` green, zero sorry/axiom.
- **End-to-end proof-generation verification (the decisive soundness check ‚Äî
  confirms native witness and target circuit agree on the new preimage; a
  mismatch would make the witness unsatisfiable):**
  `cargo test --test e2e --release` ‚Üí `e2e_deposit_validity_withdrawal` **ok
  (129s)**; `cargo test --test mle_onchain_e2e --release` ‚Üí
  `validity_proof_mle_onchain_e2e` **ok (60s, drives on-chain Forge verify)**;
  `SKIP_GROTH16=true forge test` ‚Üí **174/175 pass**. The single failing suite
  (`CloseLifecycleE2E`, a close-fixture manager/recipient address mismatch) is
  **pre-existing** ‚Äî reproduced against the committed baseline fixtures, and on
  the unrelated channel-close path, not the nullifier path.
